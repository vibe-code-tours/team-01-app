Brand Guide

Version 1.0 · The rules that keep every screen, sticker and t-shirt looking like the same company.

Fix these three things first — they cost you nothing and they're on your live site right now.
  • The name is spelled two ways. The site says Yay Thal Pya Zat; the system doc says Yay Thel Pya Zat. This guide uses Yay Thal Pya Zat. Search and replace everywhere — never mix them.
  • The footer still says "© 2026 WaterDelivery" — leftover template text. Replace with © 2026 Yay Thal Pya Zat.
  • Prices show in $ ($12 / $24 / $48) and plans are named Basic / Standard / Premium — but your system doc says Starter 5 / Regular 12 / Premium 24 coupons. Pick one product story and use it everywhere. Prices should be in MMK (Ks).

01The Idea

Everything below comes from one sentence. If a design choice doesn't serve it, don't make it.

Nobody should have to wait all day for water.

We are not a water brand. We are a delivery brand that happens to carry water. That is why the logo has motion trails, why the colours are calm-but-warm, and why the voice is short and certain. Certainty is the product.

02The Logo

A water drop, moving. The three amber marks behind it are motion trails — the drop is on its way to you.

PRIMARY — horizontal lockup
REVERSED — for navy or photo backgrounds
MARK — when the name is already nearby
APP ICON — PWA home screen & favicon
MONO — receipts, stamps, one-colour printing

Rules

✓ Always

  • Leave clear space around the logo equal to the height of the drop. Nothing enters that space.
  • Minimum size: 24 px for the mark, 120 px wide for the horizontal lockup.
  • Use the reversed (white) version on navy, teal, or any photo.
  • Use SVG on the web. Never a JPG of the logo.

✗ Never

  • Recolour the drop (no red, no green, no gradients).
  • Stretch, squash, rotate, or add a drop shadow or outline.
  • Put the navy logo on a dark or busy background.
  • Re-type the wordmark in a different font.
  • Use the 💧 emoji as a logo. That's what's on the site now — replace it.

03Colour

One colour dominates — Deep Well Navy. Teal is the water. Amber is the delivery man: the only warm, human thing on the screen. Use amber for exactly one thing per screen — the action you want taken.

The 60 / 30 / 10 rule

60% Ice + white (the page)  ·  30% Navy + teal (headers, cards, nav)  ·  10% Amber (buttons, the one thing you want tapped)

If everything is amber, nothing is. One amber button per screen.

05Type

Two fonts. Both free on Google Fonts. Both must be loaded — Burmese will not render correctly in Poppins or Inter.

Poppins — headings

Water, delivered.

Weights 600 & 700 only. Headings, buttons, prices, the wordmark.

Inter — everything else

Body text, labels, form fields, order details, receipts. Weights 400, 500, 600.

Inter is designed for small screens — which is where 95% of your customers are.

Burmese — Noto Sans Myanmar

When you add the Burmese version of the app, load Noto Sans Myanmar (or Padauk) and put it after Poppins/Inter in the font stack. The browser will use Poppins for Latin and Noto for Burmese automatically. Add ~15% more line-height for Burmese — the stacked characters need the room, or they clip.

06Voice

Short. Certain. Never clever. The whole brand promise is "you will know exactly when your water arrives" — so the writing must sound like someone who knows.

✓ Say this

  • "Thursday, 9–12. We'll be there."
  • "You have 8 coupons left."
  • "Upload your KBZPay screenshot."
  • "Payment issue — upload the slip again."
  • "Delivered. Enjoy."

✗ Not this

  • "Your hydration journey begins!"
  • "Delivery estimated within 24–72 hours."
  • "Kindly proceed to remit payment."
  • "Oops! Something went wrong 😅"
  • "Stay hydrated, stay awesome! 💧✨"
The one-sentence test: read it out loud. If it sounds like a person telling you when your water is coming, ship it. If it sounds like marketing, delete it.

08Where it goes

Digital

  • Favicon: favicon-32.png
  • PWA home-screen icon: ytpz-app-icon-192.png + -512.png in your manifest.json
  • Site header: horizontal lockup, SVG, 140px wide
  • Splash screen: stacked lockup, white on navy

Physical

  • Delivery man's shirt: mark on the chest (left), full lockup across the back. Navy shirt, white logo.
  • 20L bottle collar sticker: mark only + phone number.
  • Receipt / invoice: mono version. It has to survive a cheap thermal printer.
  • Bike / van panel: reversed lockup, as big as it fits. The trails should point in the direction of travel.
